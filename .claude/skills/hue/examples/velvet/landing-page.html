II. The Method
Eight months per chapter

Slow work, by hand, in three movements.

We do not brief outside houses, and we do not buy ready-made accords. Every material is sourced under our own contracts, then aged, decanted and finished under our own roof. Eight months is the average time from harvest to bottle. Some chapters take twice that.

I.

Harvest

We travel to the source. Cedar from the Aubrac, iris from Florence, oud from Hainan. Materials are selected at origin, then sealed and labelled by the grower before shipping.

Stage One Six origins
II.

Macerate

Raw materials rest in alcohol for sixteen weeks. Each week, Élise notes the change in character. The accord is built only after the materials have settled into themselves.

Stage Two Sixteen weeks
III.

Bottle

Bottles are filled, capped and signed by hand at the Paris atelier. Each carries an edition number, a date of bottling and the perfumer's mark. Forty per chapter, never more.

Stage Three 40 / edition
